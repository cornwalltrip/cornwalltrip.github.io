Spring Away Day Report – 23rd April, 2017

Leave a reply

West Berkshire Golf Club

The second away day of the year was held in warm and dry conditions on a course that is an old favourite for our group.
Scoring was not as high as at the birthday bash, with the victor carding 35 points. Winner Peter Harris became the second participant after Richard to take the new two shot handicap reduction . Runner up Dave Scott carded 33 points with Richard Edmonds in third with 32 – edging out Frank Tanner on countback.
Only two of the spot prizes were earned –  by Frank with NTP on 7th and Richard with longest drive at the last. Peter took the 2’s club money, also at the 7th hole.
Team wise the Scott team of Sue, Dave & Geoff took the Doom Bar award on count back – having scored 69 points. Unlucky runners up were Richard Steve Mulford and Martin Sykes.
Onwards to the main event!
Details of a July away day to follow once confirmed.

Handicaps

Leave a reply

Update and Handicap List as of July 24th.

As previously announced, we’re introducing a new handicap mechanism and have received feedback on the proposed changes. The system moving forwards is as follows:

  • Tour handicaps cannot be higher than your official Club handicap.
  • Winners of Away-Days or the Championship will have their handicap reduced by 2.
  • If your score in an Away-Day event or the Championship is more than 15 shots less than the winning score, your handicap will go up by 1.
  • The handicaps at the start of each tour will apply to the Championship, but handicaps for matchplay will be adjusted during the tour as normal (+1 for losing / -1 for winning).
  • At the end of each season (after the final Away-Day), the handicaps will be reviewed and altered based on performance during the year.

Handicaps

1 Reply

New regime for managing handicaps

2017 will see the introduction of a new mechanism for managing handicaps.
Starting with the handicaps applied at Carswell on the 19th February, the following rules will apply:
Your handicap cannot be higher than your official Club handicap.
Your handicap will be reduced by 2 shots if you win.
Your handicap will be reduced by 1 shot if your score is within 1 of the winning score.
Your handicap will increase by 1/2 a shot if your score is 8 points less than the winning score.
Your handicap will increase by 1 shot if your score is 16 points less than the winning score.
Please provide feedback on this proposal to Peter.

Early Spring Away Day Report – 19th February 2017

Leave a reply

Carswell Golf Club

The first event of 2017, celebrating four birthdays, featured 11 participants on a mild day and an apology from Dave Scott who preferred the heat of Singapore.
Scoring was good with 9 of the participants scoring in the 30’s and four on 39 points
Leading the way was Richard Edmonds with a back nine of 20 closely followed by Sue Scott (19) and Frank Tanner (18). Also on 39 was David Mitchell.
Nigel Wain and Christine Mitchell carded 37 with Dave Norman 35 + Peter Harris & Martin Sykes 34.
The team event was won by Christine, Martin, Dave N & Sue with a grand total of 93 points.
Trailing were Peter, Dave M, Frank & Reg with a score of 87 & the handicapped team of  Richard and the Wain brothers with 83.
Spot prizes went to:
David Mitchell – NTP 6th
Richard & Frank – NTP 13th   (could not be split)
Peter – NTP x2 15th
David Wain & Christine Mitchell won the longest drives on the 18th.
No birdie 2s achieved, so £11 (note Dave N) is carried forward to the next Awayday which will be held on Sunday 23rd April at West Berks.

Summer Away Day Report – 31st July 2016

Leave a reply

Ogbourne Downs Golf Club

The July away-day found a new venue at Ogbourne Downs on Sunday 31st July, kindly hosted by Christine Mitchell.
With no airshow or Open golf to distract the attendees, full focus could be applied to one’s own efforts.
The Individual event was won by bionic David Mitchell with 35 points followed by Frank Tanner with 34 and Nigel Wain on 32.
Frank and David also scooped the team event in the company of Reg brown and Micky Hubbard.
Their score of 75 points eclipsed the team of Dave Scott, Richard Edmonds, Peter Harris  and the invisible man by one point –  with Dave& Keith Norman,Nigel and Steve Mulford a further single point back.
Spot prizes went to
Mike Burton  NTP x 2 on the 3rd
Peter Harris  NTP    8th
Dave Scott Longest drive  14th
and Keith Norman NTP 17th
One birdie two was recorded by Richard Edmonds on the tricky 15th.
Special thanks to Christine for hosting the event.
